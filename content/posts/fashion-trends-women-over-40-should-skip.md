Trends can be fun, but they are not all equally wearable. The goal is not to avoid trends completely. The goal is to avoid trends that overwhelm your personal style.

## 1. Ultra-distressed denim

A small amount of distressing can work. Excessive rips and tears often look messy rather than stylish.

## 2. Head-to-toe trend dressing

When every item is trend-driven, the outfit can quickly feel dated once the trend passes.

## 3. Extremely oversized silhouettes

Volume can be elegant, but too much volume everywhere can remove shape and proportion.

## 4. Tiny micro-bags

Interesting for fashion shoots. Less useful in everyday life.

## 5. Excessive logos

Large logos often date faster than simpler designs.

## 6. Hyper-platform shoes

A statement shoe can be fun, but very exaggerated platforms can dominate an outfit.

## 7. Trend colours that fight your wardrobe

Buying colours that work with nothing else often creates expensive clutter.

## 8. Social-media-only trends

Some trends look great online and awkward in real life.

## What to do instead

Choose one trend at a time and combine it with classic pieces. That approach usually looks more expensive and lasts longer.
