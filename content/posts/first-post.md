Hair has a big effect on how current, polished and confident your overall style feels. The wrong cut or colour can make even an expensive outfit look dated.

## 1. Keeping the same haircut for decades

A signature look can be elegant. A frozen-in-time haircut usually is not. If your style has not changed since your thirties, it may be working against you.

## 2. Going too dark and flat

Very dark, single-process colour can create harsh contrast against the face. Softer dimension, subtle highlights or a slightly warmer tone often look more modern.

## 3. Over-styling every strand

Hair that is too sprayed, too curled or too perfect can feel dated. Movement usually looks fresher than stiffness.

## 4. Ignoring condition

Dry ends, dull colour and visible breakage are more ageing than grey hair itself. Shine and condition matter more than chasing a specific age-related rule.

## 5. Choosing a fringe that is too heavy

A fringe can be flattering, but a blunt, heavy fringe can close the face. Softer shaping is often easier to wear.

## 6. Fighting natural texture

Forcing hair into a shape it resists usually creates daily frustration. A cut that works with your texture looks more expensive and less forced.

## 7. Assuming long hair is always ageing

Length is not the problem. Shape, condition and styling are the issue. Long hair can look polished when it is intentional.

## 8. Assuming short hair is automatically chic

Short hair still needs shape, maintenance and styling. A short cut without structure can look severe or neglected.

## 9. Forgetting the neckline

Hair interacts with collars, scarves and jewellery. A cut that overwhelms the neckline can make outfits look crowded.

## 10. Using too much volume at the crown

A little lift is flattering. Too much height can look old-fashioned.

## 11. Never asking for a modern update

You do not need a dramatic makeover. Ask your stylist what would make your existing cut look fresher, softer or more current.

## What to do instead

Aim for hair that looks healthy, intentional and easy. Small updates often do more than a complete transformation.
