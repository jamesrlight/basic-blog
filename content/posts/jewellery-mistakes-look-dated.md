Jewellery should finish an outfit, not drag it backwards. The wrong pieces can make modern clothes look fussy, dated or overloaded.

## 1. Wearing matching jewellery sets too literally

A necklace, bracelet and earrings from the same set can look formal and old-fashioned. Mixing pieces usually feels more current.

## 2. Choosing tiny pieces that disappear

Very small jewellery can look delicate, but it can also look accidental. One visible, intentional piece often works better.

## 3. Wearing too many sentimental pieces at once

Sentimental jewellery matters, but not every meaningful item needs to be worn together. Edit the combination.

## 4. Letting tarnish show

Dull metal, cloudy stones and scratched surfaces can make an outfit look neglected.

## 5. Wearing statement necklaces with busy necklines

A strong necklace needs space. If your top already has pattern, texture or detail, earrings may be a cleaner choice.

## 6. Ignoring scale

Jewellery should work with your frame, hairstyle and outfit. Pieces that are too tiny or too oversized can throw everything off.

## 7. Keeping dated costume jewellery

Some vintage pieces are chic. Others simply look old. Be selective.

## What to do instead

Choose jewellery that looks deliberate. One strong piece is usually more stylish than several competing ones.
