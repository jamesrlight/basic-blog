Looking older is not always about age. Often, it is about clothes that feel tired, shapeless or out of step with the rest of your appearance.

## 1. Wearing everything too loose

Loose clothing can be elegant, but head-to-toe baggy pieces often remove shape completely.

## 2. Wearing everything too tight

Clothes that pull, cling or strain rarely look polished. Ease is usually more flattering than restriction.

## 3. Keeping old basics too long

Basics wear out quietly. Faded black tops, stretched T-shirts and thinning knits drag outfits down.

## 4. Choosing heavy fabrics without structure

Heavy fabric can look expensive, but only if it has shape. Without structure, it can look bulky.

## 5. Ignoring tailoring

A small alteration can make high-street clothes look far better.

## 6. Wearing dated prints

Some prints stay timeless. Others instantly place an outfit in a past decade.

## 7. Hiding behind black

Black can be chic, but too much flat black near the face can look harsh. Texture and contrast help.

## 8. Avoiding all colour

Neutrals are useful, but a wardrobe with no colour can feel severe.

## 9. Wearing the wrong hem length

Skirts and trousers that stop at awkward points can shorten the body visually.

## 10. Forgetting the waist

You do not need a tight waist, but some visual shape usually helps.

## 11. Choosing cardigans with no structure

Soft cardigans can be cosy, but limp ones can make outfits look tired.

## 12. Wearing occasion clothes during the day

Too much sheen, embellishment or formality can look misplaced.

## 13. Ignoring modern denim shapes

You do not need extreme trends, but dated denim can age an outfit quickly.

## 14. Letting sleeves ruin proportions

Sleeve length matters. Rolling, tailoring or choosing a better cut can sharpen the whole outfit.

## 15. Dressing from habit

The biggest mistake is not reviewing what still works. Your clothes should serve your current life.

## What to do instead

Aim for clothes that fit, move well and look intentional. The goal is polish, not perfection.
